FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS relating to SARS-CoV-2 Testing on Campus

Please email Professor Gibson: greg.gibson@biology.gatech.edu

No, you may leave certain questions unanswered. However, the more information you provide, the better we can help to protect your fellow students and staff.

Only a handful of individuals will ever be able to link the answers to your GT-ID or name. These are Professor Gibson, who is responsible for the study, and his study manager. They would be able to connect IDs to names, but they will be stored in different secure files.

Everyone in the study each day will receive an email inviting them to view their personal results. Once they do so, they then assent to linking their positive result to the Co-Epi app that is being used for electronic contact tracing. This is automated and involves transfer of barcodes, not names. By Law, your GT-ID will also be provided to STAMPS Student Health Center, which is obliged to then report the result and your name to the State of Georgia

Surveillance testing is designed to help slow the rate of transmission of the virus. If we know where you live and work, we can track whether multiple infections occur in the same workplace or residence, identifying a hotspot. These places will then be isolated (closing offices/labs/classrooms; requiring residents to self-quarantine) to prevent further spread.

In most cases you will be asked to report to STAMPS to ensure you get immediate and appropriate medical care. Even if you do not have any symptoms, we will ask you to move into temporary quarantine housing on campus (or to move off campus) for at least 10 days per CDC guidelines. We will ask you to help us trace your recent movements and contact friends and colleagues who may have been exposed by close contact with you, so that they can also be tested.

So long as you do not show any symptoms, 10 days is self-quarantine should be sufficient, but we will ask that you are retested and have a negative result before returning to more normal life. If you do become sick with COVID-19, your physicians will decide the best course of action.

It is currently assumed that most people who have previously tested positive will be protected for at least a few months, but there may be exceptions. You should in any case remain vigilent and continue to respect social distancing and mask wearing for the safety of others
